# HeartDiseaseData

In this repository we will examine data from a sample of males in a high-risk
heart-disease region of the Western Cape, South Africa.

# Project Purpose

The aim of this project is to create a Shiny App that allows the user to explore
factors associated with coronary heart disease in a high-risk population by
comparing lifestyle and constitutional factors. This project will help identify
which variables show the strongest associations with coronary heart disease,
providing insight into the relative importance of factors like tobacco use,
alcohol consumption, and obesity compared to genetic predisposition and age. 

# Direct Objective of Data 

The dataset records various health and behavioral risk factors for males in a
high-risk region of the Western Cape, South Africa, along with their coronary
heart disease (CHD) status. It is used to examine how lifestyle and constitutional
factors are associated with coronary heart disease and to identify which
variables are most strongly linked to disease presence.

# Raw Data Description

Each row represents one male participant from a high-risk region of the Western
Cape, South Africa, with recorded values for multiple cardiovascular risk
factors and their coronary heart disease (CHD) status (0 = no CHD, 1 = CHD). The
variables examined in this study include systolic blood pressure (sbp),
cumulative tobacco use, low density lipoprotein cholesterol (ldl), adiposity,
family history of heart disease (famhist: Present/Absent), type-A behavior,
obesity, alcohol consumption, and age at onset. These variables are recorded as
a mix of continuous numeric measures and categorical measures. The dataset
contains both CHD cases and controls, with approximately twice as many controls
as cases, and is used to compare risk factor distributions between those with
and without CHD.
